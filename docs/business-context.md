# Business Context

IronRidge has strong people and active meetings, but accountability infrastructure has not fully kept pace with growth. As departments coordinate across more work, the same action can be discussed in several settings without one durable record of owner, due date, blocker, and recovery plan.

## Current Realities

- action items often remain trapped in meeting notes
- blockers surface late when no shared queue highlights them
- weekly commitments are reviewed inconsistently across departments
- leadership lacks a clean way to see where follow-through is slipping

## Why Leaders Care

Execution drift usually appears before it becomes a project problem, but only if someone can see it. A lightweight tracker gives leaders a faster view of overdue work, blocked work, and owner accountability.
